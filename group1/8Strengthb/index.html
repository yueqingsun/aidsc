8. Strength
(Reversed)

Card Meaning and Analysis

Lack of Confidence or Self-Doubt
The reversed Strength often signifies a lack of belief in yourself. After losing your job, you may be questioning your abilities or fearing that you are not capable of handling the challenges ahead. This card highlights the need to rebuild your confidence and trust in your own skills and resilience.
Emotional Overwhelm
Strength reversed can point to emotional overwhelm or burnout. You may be struggling to cope with feelings of frustration, fear, or anger related to your job loss. These emotions could be clouding your judgment and making it harder to move forward. This card invites you to take a step back and deal with your emotions before trying to take action.
Lack of Control or Feeling Powerless
When Strength appears reversed, it can indicate that you feel out of control or powerless in your current situation. You might be experiencing a sense of helplessness or frustration, feeling as though your efforts to find a new job or move forward are not yielding results. This card suggests that it is important to regain control over your actions and emotions.
Avoiding Challenges
The reversed Strength card can also indicate that you may be avoiding confronting difficult situations or emotions. Instead of addressing your fears or doubts head-on, you may be withdrawing from challenges or procrastinating. The card urges you to face what is difficult and embrace the discomfort in order to heal and move forward.
Weakening Resolve
This card can point to a diminishing sense of willpower and perseverance. After losing your job, you may feel like giving up or losing momentum. The reversed Strength warns against giving in to despair and encourages you to regain your inner fortitude and determination.

Suggestions and Actions

Rebuild Your Confidence
Take small steps to rebuild your self-esteem. Reflect on your past successes and remind yourself of the strengths you’ve demonstrated in the past. It might help to create a list of your accomplishments, skills, and positive qualities. Seek support from friends, mentors, or therapists to help restore your belief in yourself.
Take Time for Emotional Healing
It’s important to address your emotional well-being during this time. Strength reversed suggests that you may need to pause and give yourself permission to feel your emotions without judgment. Take time to process any feelings of frustration, anger, or fear that may have arisen from losing your job. Self-care practices like journaling, meditation, or speaking to a trusted confidant can help.
Regain Control
If you’re feeling out of control, focus on areas where you can take action. Set small, manageable goals for yourself, whether it's updating your resume, researching job opportunities, or learning new skills. By taking proactive steps, you will regain a sense of control over your situation.
Confront Your Fears
The reversed Strength asks you to confront your fears rather than avoiding them. Are you afraid of rejection, failure, or uncertainty about the future? Acknowledge these fears and take steps to face them. For example, you might start by applying for jobs that feel slightly outside your comfort zone or seeking guidance from someone in your desired field.
Practice Patience and Self-Compassion
Remember to be kind to yourself. Strength reversed may indicate that you’re being too hard on yourself. Job loss is a difficult experience, and it’s okay to take your time to heal. Practice patience with yourself, and give yourself credit for the efforts you’re making, even if things aren’t moving as quickly as you’d like.

Final Thought

Strength reversed signals a time of emotional struggle and self-doubt, but it also offers an opportunity for growth. By acknowledging and addressing your fears and emotional overwhelm, you can rebuild your confidence and tap into your inner power. This period of reflection and self-compassion will ultimately help you regain the resilience you need to move forward and face challenges with renewed strength. Be patient with yourself—your inner strength is still there, just waiting to be reawakened.
