21. The World
(Reversed)

Card Meaning and Analysis

Incomplete Cycles
The World reversed indicates that something is left unresolved. This could relate to unfinished business in your previous job, lingering emotions like regret or frustration, or personal growth that hasn’t been fully realized. You may need to address these unresolved issues to move forward.
Feeling Stuck
The reversed card often reflects a sense of stagnation or being unable to progress. You might feel like you’re not moving toward your goals, or that external circumstances are holding you back. This could stem from a lack of clarity or direction in your career path.
Missed Opportunities
The World reversed suggests that you may be overlooking opportunities for growth or change. You might be clinging to the past or holding onto old habits, preventing you from fully embracing the new chapter that lies ahead.
Self-Limiting Beliefs
This card can indicate that self-doubt or fear of failure is stopping you from achieving your goals. You may feel overwhelmed by the idea of starting over or unsure of your ability to succeed in a new role or field.
Delayed Success
While upright The World symbolizes completion and achievement, its reversal suggests delays. Success may still be within reach, but you need to address the obstacles or mindset keeping you from fully realizing it.

Suggestions and Actions

Resolve Unfinished Business
Reflect on any unresolved issues from your previous job. Are there skills you still need to develop, or lessons you’ve yet to learn? Take time to tie up loose ends emotionally and mentally so you can start your next chapter with clarity and focus.
Identify Blockages
Consider what’s holding you back. Are you afraid of change? Do you feel unprepared for a new opportunity? Identifying these obstacles can help you create a plan to overcome them. Journaling or talking with a mentor or counselor might provide valuable insights.
Reassess Your Goals
The World reversed suggests it’s time to revisit your long-term goals. Are they still aligned with your values and passions? If not, adjust them to reflect the person you’ve become. This reevaluation can give you the motivation and direction you need.
Take Small Steps Forward
If you’re feeling stuck, focus on taking small, manageable steps rather than trying to make a huge leap. Update your resume, apply for a few jobs, or reach out to your network. These small actions can help build momentum and confidence.
Release Limiting Beliefs
The reversed World can highlight self-imposed limitations. Challenge any negative beliefs about yourself or your abilities. Remember that losing a job doesn’t define your worth—it’s simply a moment in your journey, and one that can lead to growth.
Be Patient
Delays don’t mean failure. The reversed World suggests that success is still possible, but it may take time. Stay focused on your efforts, trust the process, and remain patient as you work toward your goals.

Final Thought

The World reversed is a reminder that while you may feel stuck or incomplete, the potential for growth and success is still within reach. By addressing unresolved issues, identifying and overcoming blockages, and taking small but meaningful steps, you can move toward a more fulfilling and complete chapter of your career. Trust in the process and remain open to the possibilities ahead, even if the path forward feels delayed or uncertain.
